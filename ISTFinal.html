SoundCloud

  • About -

    SoundCloud is a social audio platform that allows users to create and share sounds.
    Who? -Created by two Swedes, Eric Wahlforss and Alexander Ljung.
    When? - 2008
    Where? - Berlin, Germany

  • Created for -

    SoundCloud was created solely for artists to promote and share their music with people. Community that mostly uses it are labels, artists, dj’s, producers, promoters, podcasters, and listeners. Politicians, news media, cable channels, publishers, and educational users in Yale, Columbia University, Stanford, and University College London also use it to share podcasts.
    Today SoundCloud is still used for the same thing and continues to grow to become more social.

SoundCloud is the world's leading music and audio discovery platform. Because it is free to sign up and post your work, it gives up and coming artists a great opportunity to be discovered and share their creations. For the year of 2019 the company’s gross revenue run-rate exceeded $200 million.

Challenges for Artists Pockets

So it’s only fair that after reaching so many streams or likes/reposts they should take home some of the profit for their hard work right? Wrong. That is until REPOST by SoundCloud came out on April 2, 2020. Until now SoundCloud has been viewed as a marketing tool and not to generate revenue.

SoundCloud gives the artist the spotlight to get in front of audiences and the platform to start their journey. Now with REPOST that’s all been changed and artists can earn money for their SoundCloud plays.

How it Works:

Because SoundCloud was created with the idea of sharing music there are certain rights that need to be set. Copyright is automatically granted to artists who publish their creative work on SoundCloud. However, you need to ask yourself these questions first.

Can you answer "yes" to all of the following questions?
  • Did you compose the music yourself?
  • Did you write the lyrics yourself?
  • Did you record and produce the track yourself or do you have permission from the producer or record label that made the recording?
  • Did you record and produce the track yourself or do you have permission from the producer or record label that made the recording?
Can you answer "no" to all of the following questions?
  • Were you signed to a record label when you recorded the track?
  • Do you have a publishing deal?
  • Are you a member of a performing rights organization or collecting society?
  • Have you licensed your track to anyone else?
  • Does the track contain the entirety or any part of someone else’s song(s) Is it based on someone else’s song(s)?

A Culture Within a Culture:

If we look at the culture of music as a whole, SoundCloud has created its own “subculture” by making it more of a social platform. It gives users the sense that they are part of a community, by reposting and commenting, and have the freedom to create their own “sound cultures”. The most popular “sound cultures” that we have seen emerge from the rise of SoundCloud are electronic music and hip hop (SoundCloud rappers).

“the most successful online communities, like SoundCloud, have the feel of public spaces, where everyone can contribute to the culture. They feel as if they belong to the community that sustains them.”

Jenna Wortham

If SoundCloud Disappears, What Happens to Its Music Culture?

How SoundCloud has Redifined the Culture of the Music World:

SoundCloud is an online music sharing platform that allows lesser known artists to gather attention from either other artists who want to collaborate or record labels looking to sign someone. The community-first approach SoundCloud takes is why artists fall in love with this platform over others because they aren’t tied down by a label saying they can’t release music when they want to. SoundCloud then benefits from this fact as well. Spotify is found having to give over 50% of their revenue back to the labels for licensing fees. SoundCloud has created an international culture of music that can’t be found anywhere else online.
