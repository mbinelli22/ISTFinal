SoundCloud

  • About -

    SoundCloud is a social audio platform that allows users to create and share sounds.
    Who? -Created by two Swedes, Eric Wahlforss and Alexander Ljung.
    When? - 2008
    Where? - Berlin, Germany

  • Created for -

    SoundCloud was created solely for artists to promote and share their music with people. Community that mostly uses it are labels, artists, dj’s, producers, promoters, podcasters, and listeners. Politicians, news media, cable channels, publishers, and educational users in Yale, Columbia University, Stanford, and University College London also use it to share podcasts.
    Today SoundCloud is still used for the same thing and continues to grow to become more social.

SoundCloud is the world's leading music and audio discovery platform. Because it is free to sign up and post your work, it gives up and coming artists a great opportunity to be discovered and share their creations. For the year of 2019 the company’s gross revenue run-rate exceeded $200 million.

Challenges for Artists

So it’s only fair that after reaching so many streams or likes/reposts they should take home some of the profit for their hard work right? Wrong. That is until REPOST by SoundCloud came out on April 2, 2020. Until now SoundCloud has been viewed as a marketing tool and not to generate revenue.

SoundCloud gives the artist the spotlight to get in front of audiences and the platform to start their journey. Now with REPOST that’s all been changed and artists can earn money for their SoundCloud plays.
